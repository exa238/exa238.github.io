Introduction

Malawi shares its name with the Chewa word for flames and is linked to the Maravi people from whom the Chewa language originated. The Maravi settled in what is now Malawi around 1400 during one of the later waves of Bantu migration across Central and Southern Africa. Several of Malawi’s ethnic groups trace their origins to different Maravi lineages
